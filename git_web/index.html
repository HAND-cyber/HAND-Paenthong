Hi, I’m Worathat Paenthong,
a Ph.D. Student at SOKENDAI.

I study energetic particle confinement in the Large Helical Device (LHD) using a newly developed Imaging Neutral Particle Analyzer (INPA), combining experiments and simulations to deepen our understanding of fusion plasma behavior.

Portfolio

I am passionate about:

  • Python coding for data analysis and visualization
  • Fast neutron detectors and related diagnostics
  • Imaging Neutral Particle Analyzer (INPA) development
  • Machine learning and deep learning applications

Education

I have a strong academic background in physics and fusion science, with research experience spanning both Thailand and Japan. Here’s a summary of my educational journey:

  • Ph.D. in Fusion Science – SOKENDAI, Japan (2023–Present)
    Research Topic: Focused on energetic particle confinement in the Large Helical Device (LHD) using the newly developed Imaging Neutral Particle Analyzer (INPA).
  • M.Sc. in Physics – King Mongkut’s University of Technology Thonburi (KMUTT), Thailand (2021–2023)
    Research Topic: Fast-ion orbit analysis in Thailand Tokamak-1.
  • B.Sc. in Applied Physics – King Mongkut’s University of Technology Thonburi (KMUTT), Thailand (2017–2021)
    Research Topic: Multi-channel resonance spectroscopy for Volatile organic compounds (VOCs) optical sensor array.
